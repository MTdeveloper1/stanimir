About me

I was born in Sofia, Bulgaria in 1967. I started studying cello at the National School of Music and from 1988 to 1991 I was a student of Professor Zdravko Jordanov at the State Academy of Music. for In 1991, I was awarded a further scholarship at the International Menuhin Music Academy (IMMA) in Gstaad, Switzerland. I was assistant to Professor Aldulescu at IMMA and joined Camerata Lysy Gstaad as first cello.

Works

I was a professor at the Royal Conservatory of Music of Copenhagen, Denmark of the Music Conservatory of Malmö, Sweden and of the IMMA (Switzerland). In 1994 I was awarded at the International Rostropovich competition in Paris, where I won an award for the best performance of a work by Alfred Schnittke. He was the first desk of the Danish National Radio and Suisse Romande orchestras. for I was the first cello of the Monte Carlo Philharmonic Orchestra and since 2009 I have been a member of the Stable Orchestra of the Teatro Colón. I taught classes in Spain, Guatemala, Bolivia, South Africa, Bulgaria and Argentina.
